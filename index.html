      Know the red flags

      🔑 Nobody legitimate asks for your OTP or PIN

      Not your bank, not the police, not "customer support". An OTP is the key to your money — anyone asking for it is stealing from you. This single rule stops most frauds.

      ⏰ Urgency is a weapon

      "Account blocked in 24 hours", "offer expires now" — pressure exists to stop you from thinking or asking someone. Real institutions never need you to panic.

      🔗 Check the real domain, not the look

      Scam links imitate banks: sbi-verify.xyz, paypa1.com, secure.hdfc.account-check.top. The only part that matters is the last domain before the first "/" — and it must be the bank's own.

      💸 You never pay to receive money

      No fee unlocks a prize, refund, loan, or parcel. And on UPI, receiving money never requires approving a request or entering your PIN.

      📱 Apps only from official stores

      APK files sent on WhatsApp/SMS bypass security checks. Fake "support" and loan apps read your messages to steal OTPs and contacts.

      💬 WhatsApp's favourite scams

      "Hi mum, this is my new number" + an urgent transfer, job offers that start with a group-invite link, and "I sent my 6-digit code to you by mistake — send it back" (that code hands over your WhatsApp account). Share any of these to FinShield before replying.

      If you've already been scammed

      • Call your bank's official number (on the back of your card) and freeze the card/account immediately.
      • In India, report within the golden hour: dial 1930 or file at cybercrime.gov.in.
      • Change passwords for any account whose details you shared, and enable two-factor authentication.
      • If you installed an APK or gave remote access, disconnect from the internet and uninstall it; consider a factory reset.
